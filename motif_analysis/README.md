# Conserved Motif Analysis of CBL and CIPK Proteins in Fagopyrum esculentum

## 🧬 Objective
To identify and annotate conserved sequence motifs in buckwheat CBL (Calcineurin B-like) and CIPK (CBL-interacting protein kinase) proteins using MEME, and to relate these motifs to known functional domains.

---

## 📊 Input Data
- Non-redundant CBL protein sequences (buckwheat)
- Non-redundant CIPK protein sequences (buckwheat)
- Sequences previously validated using BLASTp and InterProScan

---

## ⚙️ Methodology

### MEME Analysis (CBL Proteins)
- Sequence type: Protein  
- Motif model: ZOOPS (Zero or One Occurrence Per Sequence)  
- Number of motifs: 5  
- Motif width: 6–50 amino acids  
- Background model: 0-order Markov  

### MEME Analysis (CIPK Proteins)
- Sequence type: Protein  
- Motif model: ZOOPS  
- Number of motifs: 10  
- Motif width: 6–50 amino acids  
- Background model: 0-order Markov  

---

## 🔬 Results

### CBL Proteins
- Five conserved motifs identified  
- Motifs correspond to EF-hand calcium-binding domains  
- Motif architecture highly conserved across all CBL proteins  
- No kinase-related motifs detected  

### CIPK Proteins
- Ten conserved motifs identified  
- Motifs mapped to:
  - N-terminal kinase domain (ATP-binding, catalytic regions)  
  - C-terminal NAF/FISL regulatory domain  
- Motif distribution consistent across CIPK proteins  

---

## 🧩 Motif Annotation

### CBL Motifs
- Majority overlap with EF-hand domains  
- Represent calcium-binding and sensing regions  
- Highly conserved across all members  

### CIPK Motifs
- Multiple motifs mapped to kinase subdomains  
- One motif corresponds to NAF/FISL domain (CBL interaction)  
- Some motifs show no known domain overlap (potential novel regions)  

---

## 🧠 Interpretation
- CBL proteins show conserved calcium-binding motif architecture  
- CIPK proteins show conserved kinase and regulatory motifs  
- Motif-domain correspondence confirms functional classification  
- Conserved patterns indicate evolutionary conservation of CBL–CIPK signaling
